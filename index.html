Housing and Shelter
A safe place to sleep tonight.
Emergency shelter available 24/7, regardless of documentation status.

Emergency shelter is available to anyone in New York City — no ID, no papers, no prior address needed. Call 311 at any hour and say "shelter." Families with children are given priority and kept together.

What to know

  • Call 311 any time, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week — say "shelter"
  • No documentation required at any city shelter
  • If you have an IRC case number, contact them first for faster placement
  • Families are always kept together and given priority

Food and Basic Needs
Free food for your family.
400+ food pantries and meal programs across all five boroughs. No docs needed.

Over 400 free food pantries operate across New York City. Most require no documentation. Search cityharvest.org by zip code to find the nearest pantry that is open right now.

What to know

  • Most pantries require no documentation, ID, or proof of address
  • Search cityharvest.org by zip code to find hours and location near you
  • Many pantries also provide baby supplies, feminine products, and household essentials
  • SNAP (food stamps) may be available — ask a caseworker if you may qualify

Legal Aid and Immigration
Free legal help for your case.
Asylum, green cards, DACA, citizenship — free regardless of your status.

You have rights in New York City regardless of your immigration status. Free legal services are available for asylum, green cards, DACA, citizenship, and deportation defense. You will not be reported to immigration authorities for seeking legal help.

Know your rights

  • You have the right to remain silent with immigration officers
  • Never sign anything you do not understand — ask for a translator first
  • All organizations below are confidential — they will not report you
  • If you are unsure where to start, call NYIC at (212) 627-2227 for a referral

English Classes and Education
Learn English for free.
Classes at every level across all five boroughs. No tuition or registration fee.

Free English classes are available at public libraries, community colleges, and nonprofits across all five boroughs. No tuition, no application fee, no prior education requirement. Many offer evening and weekend schedules for people who work.

Getting started

  • NYC Public Library ESL classes require no registration fee — many branches let you walk in and start
  • Tell the program your current English level — most have beginner, intermediate, and advanced tracks
  • Many programs also offer GED and high school equivalency preparation
  • Some programs offer childcare during class — ask when you call

Healthcare and Mental Health
Care for your body and mind.
Full medical care regardless of status. Mental health support available 24/7.

NYC Health + Hospitals serves everyone regardless of immigration status or ability to pay. You will not be reported to immigration authorities for seeking care. Mental health support is available 24 hours a day in multiple languages.

What to know

  • NYC Health + Hospitals treats everyone — payment is based on income, many pay nothing
  • Safe Horizon is available 24/7 in multiple languages for crisis support
  • Calling or texting 988 connects you to free mental health support any hour
  • All services listed are confidential — your status will not be shared

Why this exists

Built for everyone who arrives without a guide.

My mother's family came to this country as refugees from Laos. By elementary school she was already translating legal documents for my grandparents — navigating systems she barely understood, in a language she was still learning, without anyone to show her where to go.

This tool is what I wish had existed for her. Available 24 hours a day, asking nothing from you, connecting you to real organizations with real addresses and real phone numbers. You do not need papers to use it. You do not need to explain yourself.

Nothing you type is stored. Nothing is shared. This is a safe space to find help.
